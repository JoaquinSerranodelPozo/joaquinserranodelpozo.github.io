Historian of Late Antique and Eastern Roman (“Byzantine”) culture, medieval war, Christian relics, and sacred objects.

About

I am a historian specialising in Late Antique and Eastern Roman (“Byzantine”) culture. My research interests include the study of medieval war from a sociocultural perspective, the veneration of Christian relics, and the interplay between literary, material, and iconographic sources.

Originally from Chile, I completed a BA and an MSt in History at the Pontifical Catholic University of Valparaíso, before moving to Scotland for a PhD at the Centre for Late Antique, Islamic and Byzantine Studies at the University of Edinburgh.

After completing my PhD, I moved to Tübingen for a Teach@Tübingen fellowship. In 2025, I was awarded funding by the DFG for a two-year Walter Benjamin postdoctoral position at the University of Tübingen and Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz.

Research

Relics, war, and political authority

My doctoral thesis examined the use of Christian relics in Late Roman and Byzantine war. My current postdoctoral research expands this topic across the medieval Euro-Mediterranean world, c. 600–1200.

Texts, objects, and images

I am interested in how literary, material, and iconographic sources can be read together to reconstruct the social and political meanings of sacred objects.

Teaching

I have taught Roman, Byzantine, and art history at the University of Edinburgh and the University of Tübingen. In Tübingen, I taught the course “The End of Antiquity? The Crucial Seventh Century and Byzantium.”

Español

Soy historiador especializado en la cultura tardoantigua y romano-oriental (“bizantina”). Mis intereses de investigación incluyen el estudio sociocultural de la guerra medieval, la veneración de reliquias cristianas y la relación entre fuentes literarias, materiales e iconográficas.

Mi tesis doctoral examinó el uso de reliquias cristianas en la guerra tardo-romana y bizantina. Mi investigación postdoctoral actual amplía este tema al uso político y militar de reliquias cristianas por gobernantes de distintos reinos y entidades políticas del mundo euromediterráneo medieval, c. 600–1200.
